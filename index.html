Chapters 5, 5.3 & 6.1: Free Body Diagrams, Friction & Equilibrium

Friction (F_f)

Guided Learning

Step 1: Flat Surface

Let's start simple. A block resting on a flat surface.

  1. Make sure Angle is 0°.
  2. Check "Mass (Mg) & R_N".
  3. Toggle "Show Equations".

Key Concepts:

  • Weight (Mg) acts vertically down.
  • Normal Force (R_N) is perpendicular to the surface.
  • Forces are in equilibrium:
    ΣFy = +R_N - Mg = 0, so R_N = Mg.

Step 2: Flat Surface (Friction)

Now, let's try to move the block.

  1. Keep angle at 0°.
  2. Add "Tension (T)".
  3. Select "Impending Motion Up-Slope" (i.e., "to the right").

Question: The block is *about* to move right. What is the direction of the friction force (F_f)?

Step 3: Inclined Plane

This is the most important part. Uncheck all forces first.

  1. Click "Reset All".
  2. Set Angle to 30°.
  3. Check "Mass (Mg) & R_N".

Question 1: What is the direction of the Weight (Mg)?

Step 4: Resolving Weight

Our axes (x' and y') are tilted. The Weight (Mg) is not on either axis. We must resolve it into components.

  1. Note the components: Mg·sin(θ) (parallel) and Mg·cos(θ) (perpendicular).
  2. Toggle "Show Equations".

Question: Look at the ΣFy equation. Which component of weight does the Normal Force (R_N) balance?

Step 5: Equilibrium on Incline

The component Mg·sin(θ) is trying to pull the block *down* the incline. Let's add friction to stop it.

  1. Select "Impending Motion Down-Slope".

Question: To prevent the block from sliding down, what direction must the friction force (F_f) act?

Module Complete!

You've mastered the core concepts!

  • Normal Force (R_N) is *perpendicular* to the surface.
  • Weight (Mg) is *vertically down*.
  • Weight is resolved into Mg·cos(θ) and Mg·sin(θ).
  • Friction (F_f) *opposes* impending motion.

You are now in "Free Play" mode.

Note on Ch 5.3: For forces at an angle (like the 120N example), you must first resolve that force into its *own* parallel (x') and perpendicular (y') components. Then, add them to the ΣFx and ΣFy equations.

Equations

ΣFy = 0 (Perpendicular)

+ R_N - Mg·cos(θ) - T·sin(α) + P·sin(β) = 0

ΣFx = 0 (Parallel)

+ T - P + F_f - F_f - Mg·sin(θ) = 0
